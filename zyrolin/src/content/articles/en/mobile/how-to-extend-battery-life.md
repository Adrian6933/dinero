Battery anxiety is real, but a few changes can add hours to your phone's life. Here's what actually works.

## The biggest battery drains

| Drain | Fix |
|---|---|
| Screen brightness | Use auto-brightness, lower it |
| Background apps | Restrict background refresh |
| Location services | Set to "while using" only |
| Push email | Switch to manual/scheduled fetch |
| 5G in weak areas | Use 4G when signal is poor |

## Step 1: Lower and automate brightness

The screen is the #1 battery drain. Turn on auto-brightness and keep it modest.

## Step 2: Manage background activity

Stop apps from refreshing in the background unless you need them to. On iPhone: Settings → General → Background App Refresh.

## Step 3: Use battery saver mode

Both Android and iPhone have a low-power mode that pauses non-essential activity. Turn it on when you're below 30%.

## Step 4: Protect long-term battery health

- Avoid letting it hit 0% regularly.
- Don't leave it charging to 100% overnight constantly.
- Keep it out of extreme heat — heat permanently damages batteries.

## FAQ

**Does closing apps save battery?**
No. Force-closing and reopening apps actually uses more battery.

**Is it bad to charge overnight?**
Occasional is fine; modern phones stop at 100%. But daily overnight charging slowly ages the battery.

## Conclusion

Lower your brightness, limit background activity, use battery saver, and protect battery health. Small habits, hours of extra life.
