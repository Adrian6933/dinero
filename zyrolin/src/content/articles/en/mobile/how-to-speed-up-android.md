Android phones slow down over time as apps, cache, and background processes pile up. Here's how to bring back the speed.

## Step 1: Free up storage

A nearly full phone runs slowly. Go to **Settings → Storage** and delete unused apps, old downloads, and clear cached files.

## Step 2: Limit background apps

Many apps run in the background draining resources. In **Settings → Apps**, restrict background activity for apps you rarely use.

## Step 3: Turn off animations

In Developer Options, set window, transition, and animator scales to 0.5x. The phone instantly feels snappier.

> To enable Developer Options: Settings → About phone → tap "Build number" seven times.

## Step 4: Uninstall or disable bloatware

Remove apps you never use. Pre-installed apps you can't uninstall can often be **disabled**.

## Step 5: Keep it updated

System and app updates often include performance fixes. Keep both up to date.

## FAQ

**Do task killers help?**
No — modern Android manages memory itself. Task killers can actually slow things down.

**Should I factory reset?**
Only as a last resort. The steps above fix most slowdowns.

## Conclusion

Free up storage, limit background apps, reduce animations, and remove bloatware. Your Android will feel years younger.
