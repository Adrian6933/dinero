Setting up a new phone used to mean losing things. Now, with a backup and the right tool, you can move almost everything — photos, contacts, apps, even messages. Here's how, whichever phones you have.

## Step 1: Back up your old phone first

Before anything, back up. It's your safety net if the transfer hiccups.

- **iPhone:** Settings → your name → iCloud → iCloud Backup.
- **Android:** Settings → Google → Backup.

## Step 2: Transfer between the same brand

This is the easiest case — the built-in setup wizard does the heavy lifting:

| From → To | Use |
|---|---|
| iPhone → iPhone | Quick Start (hold phones close) |
| Android → Android | The "Copy apps & data" setup step |
| Old → new (same Google/Apple ID) | Restore from your backup |

Just keep both phones charged and on Wi-Fi during the move.

## Step 3: Switch between Android and iPhone

Crossing platforms works too, using the official apps:

- **Android → iPhone:** use Apple's "Move to iOS" app during iPhone setup.
- **iPhone → Android:** use your new phone's transfer tool or cable, plus Google Photos for pictures.

> **Tip:** Messages and some app data are the trickiest to move across platforms. Back up chat apps (like WhatsApp) separately using their own backup feature.

## Step 4: Check everything moved

Before wiping the old phone, confirm your photos, contacts, and important apps are all on the new one and that you're logged in to your accounts.

## FAQ

**Will my apps come over?**
Apps reinstall from the store; most app *data* transfers if you used a backup or same-brand transfer. Log in to each app to be sure.

**Do I need a cable?**
Usually no — most transfers work over Wi-Fi device-to-device. A cable can be faster for large amounts of data.

## Conclusion

Back up first, use the built-in tool for same-brand phones or the official app to cross platforms, then verify everything arrived before wiping the old phone. A smooth move with nothing lost.
