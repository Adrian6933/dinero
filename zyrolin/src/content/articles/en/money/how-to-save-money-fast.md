Whether you're building an emergency fund or saving for something specific, these tips help you save money fast — without giving up everything you enjoy.

## Quick wins (do these today)

1. **Cancel unused subscriptions.** Check your bank statement — most people pay for at least one they forgot about.
2. **Set up an automatic transfer** to savings on payday so you save before you spend.
3. **Use a 24-hour rule** for non-essential purchases to curb impulse buying.

## Cut recurring costs

4. Renegotiate your phone and internet bills — a 10-minute call can save hundreds a year.
5. Switch to a cheaper energy plan or provider.
6. Review insurance policies annually and shop around.

## Spend smarter on food

7. Plan meals weekly and shop with a list.
8. Cook in batches and freeze portions.
9. Bring lunch instead of buying it.

## Earn a little extra

10. Sell things you don't use.
11. Pick up occasional freelance or gig work.
12. Put any windfalls (bonuses, refunds) straight into savings.

## How much can this save?

| Tip | Potential monthly saving |
|---|---|
| Cancel subscriptions | $20–60 |
| Lower bills | $30–80 |
| Meal planning | $100–200 |

## FAQ

**What should I save for first?**
A small emergency fund (even $500–1,000) comes before everything else — it stops one surprise from derailing you.

## Conclusion

Pick three tips from this list and start today. Small, consistent changes add up faster than you'd think.
