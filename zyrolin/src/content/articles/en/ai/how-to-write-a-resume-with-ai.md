AI can turn a blank page into a strong resume draft in minutes. The catch is that AI defaults to generic, robotic phrasing — so your job is to guide it and then make it sound human. Here's the process.

## Step 1: Give the AI the right inputs

A vague prompt gives a vague resume. Feed it:

- Your real experience, skills, and achievements.
- The **job description** you're applying for.
- The tone you want (professional, concise).

## Step 2: Ask it to tailor, not generalize

Generic resumes get ignored. Paste the job description and ask the AI to match your experience to the specific requirements and keywords in it.

| Weak ask | Strong ask |
|---|---|
| "Write me a resume" | "Rewrite my experience to match this job description, mirroring its key skills" |

## Step 3: Lead with results

The biggest upgrade to any resume is replacing duties with results. Ask the AI to rewrite each bullet to start with an action and include a number.

> **Tip:** "Responsible for sales" is weak. "Grew sales 30% in 6 months" gets read. Always quantify when you can.

## Step 4: Make it sound human

AI phrasing is often bland and over-formal. Read every line aloud and cut buzzwords ("synergy", "results-driven"). Replace anything you'd never actually say.

## Step 5: Beat the filters and proofread

Many companies use software (ATS) that scans for keywords from the job post. Make sure relevant terms appear naturally. Then proofread carefully — AI can invent details, so verify every fact is true.

## FAQ

**Will employers know I used AI?**
If you edit it to sound like you and keep everything truthful, it's just a well-written resume. Don't submit raw AI output — it reads generic.

**Is it okay to let AI write achievements?**
Use AI to *word* your real achievements better — never to invent ones you don't have. Lies get caught in interviews.

## Conclusion

Feed the AI your real experience and the job description, tailor each application, lead with quantified results, and edit until it sounds like you. AI speeds up the writing — your judgment makes it land.
