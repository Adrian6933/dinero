There are thousands of AI tools, but only a handful are worth your time. Here are the most useful categories and what to look for in each.

## For writing and chat

A general AI assistant handles emails, summaries, brainstorming, and questions. Look for one with a strong free tier and a good mobile app.

## For images

AI image generators turn text into visuals. Choose one based on your need: realism, artistic style, or readable text in designs.

## For video

AI video tools can create talking-avatar presenters or edit footage automatically — great for faceless content creators.

## Quick comparison

| Category | Best for | Free option? |
|---|---|---|
| Chat assistant | Everyday tasks | Yes |
| Image generator | Visuals & art | Limited |
| Video/avatar | Content creation | Limited |
| Coding assistant | Developers | Limited |
| Transcription | Notes & subtitles | Yes |

## How to choose

1. Define the one task you need help with most.
2. Try the free tier before paying.
3. Check the licensing if you'll use the output commercially.

## FAQ

**Do I need to pay for AI tools?**
Often no — free tiers handle most everyday needs. Pay only when you hit real limits.

**Are AI tools safe to use for work?**
Avoid pasting confidential data, and check your company's policy first.

## Conclusion

Start with one strong chat assistant, add an image tool when you need visuals, and only pay once a free tool can't keep up. Match the tool to the task, not the hype.
