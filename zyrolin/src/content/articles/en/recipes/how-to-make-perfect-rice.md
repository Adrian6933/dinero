Perfectly fluffy rice is one of the most useful skills in the kitchen — and one of the most commonly messed up. Here's a foolproof method that works with just a pot.

## The golden ratio

For most white rice, use **1 cup of rice to 1.5 cups of water**. This ratio is the single most important thing to get right.

| Rice type | Rice : Water | Cook time |
|---|---|---|
| White (long grain) | 1 : 1.5 | 15 min |
| Basmati | 1 : 1.5 | 15 min |
| Brown | 1 : 2.25 | 40 min |
| Jasmine | 1 : 1.25 | 12 min |

## Step 1: Rinse the rice

Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs (mostly) clear. This removes excess starch that makes rice gummy.

## Step 2: Bring to a boil

Add the rice and water to a pot with a pinch of salt. Bring it to a boil over medium-high heat, uncovered.

## Step 3: Simmer covered

Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover with a tight lid, and let it simmer. **Don't lift the lid** — you need the trapped steam.

## Step 4: Rest, then fluff

When the time is up, turn off the heat and let it sit covered for 5 minutes. Then fluff gently with a fork. This rest step is what makes it light and separate.

## FAQ

**Why is my rice mushy?**
Too much water or stirring during cooking. Stick to the ratio and don't stir.

**Why is it crunchy?**
Not enough water or the heat was too high. Add a splash of water, cover, and cook a few more minutes.

## Conclusion

Rinse, use the right ratio, simmer covered, and rest. Master those four steps and you'll never ruin rice again.
