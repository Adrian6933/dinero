Homemade pizza dough costs pennies, beats delivery, and needs only four ingredients. The secret isn't skill — it's letting time do the work. Here's a foolproof recipe.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Flour (bread flour is best) | 500 g |
| Warm water | 325 ml |
| Dry yeast | 7 g (1 packet) |
| Salt | 10 g |

## Step 1: Mix

Stir the yeast into the warm water and let it sit 5 minutes until foamy. Add the flour and salt, then mix until a shaggy dough forms with no dry flour left.

## Step 2: Knead

Knead on a lightly floured surface for about 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. It should spring back when poked. (A stand mixer works too.)

## Step 3: Let it rise

Put the dough in an oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise until doubled — 1–2 hours at room temperature.

> **Tip:** For the best flavor, let it rise slowly in the fridge overnight. A long, cold rise develops far more taste and a chewier crust.

## Step 4: Shape and bake

Divide into 2 balls, stretch each by hand (don't use a rolling pin — it pushes out the air), top, and bake on the hottest setting your oven allows. A preheated baking tray or stone gives a crispier base.

## FAQ

**Can I use regular all-purpose flour?**
Yes, it works fine. Bread flour has more protein, giving a chewier crust, but all-purpose is perfectly good.

**Why is my dough tough?**
Usually too much flour or not enough rest. Let the dough relax before stretching, and don't over-flour it.

## Conclusion

Mix, knead, let it rise (overnight if you can), then stretch and bake hot. Four cheap ingredients and a little patience make pizza that beats takeout.
