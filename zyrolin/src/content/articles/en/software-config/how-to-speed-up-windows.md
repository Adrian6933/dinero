Beyond the basics, Windows has hidden settings that can make your PC noticeably snappier. Here are the safe, high-impact ones.

## Step 1: Disable startup apps

Open Task Manager (**Ctrl + Shift + Esc**) → **Startup apps**, and disable anything you don't need at boot. This is the single biggest win for boot speed.

## Step 2: Turn off visual animations

Search **"Adjust the appearance and performance of Windows"** and choose **Adjust for best performance**, or disable specific animations. The UI instantly feels faster.

## Step 3: Set the High Performance power plan

In **Control Panel → Power Options**, choose **High Performance** (or Ultimate Performance) so your CPU isn't held back.

## Step 4: Free up startup with Storage Sense

Enable **Storage Sense** (Settings → System → Storage) to automatically clear temporary files that pile up.

## Step 5: Disable background apps

In **Settings → Apps**, stop apps you rarely use from running in the background and eating resources.

> Create a System Restore point before deeper tweaks so you can undo changes easily.

## FAQ

**Is it safe to disable startup apps?**
Yes — keep antivirus enabled, but most others (updaters, chat apps) are fine to disable.

**Will these tweaks break anything?**
The ones above are safe. Avoid random "registry hacks" from unknown sources.

## Conclusion

Trim startup apps, cut animations, use a high-performance power plan, and clear temp files. Safe tweaks that add up to a faster Windows.
