Overpacking is the most common travel mistake. The trick isn't a bigger suitcase — it's a smarter method. Here's how to fit more, find things easily, and arrive wrinkle-free.

## Step 1: Plan a capsule wardrobe

Before packing anything, plan outfits around **one color scheme** so everything mixes and matches. You'll pack far less and still have plenty to wear.

## Step 2: Roll, fold, or cube — strategically

Different items pack best different ways:

| Item type | Best method |
|---|---|
| T-shirts, casual wear | Roll (saves space) |
| Shirts, blazers | Fold (avoids creases) |
| Underwear, socks | Stuff into shoes/gaps |
| Everything | Packing cubes to organize |

## Step 3: Layer by weight

Build your suitcase in layers: **heavy items (shoes, toiletries) at the bottom near the wheels**, then folded structured clothes, then rolled clothes on top. This keeps the bag balanced and protects delicate items.

> **Tip:** Stuff socks and chargers inside your shoes — it saves space and helps shoes keep their shape.

## Step 4: Protect and separate

- Put liquids in a sealed bag (carry-on rule: containers ≤100ml in one clear bag).
- Keep one outfit and essentials in your carry-on in case checked luggage is delayed.
- Use a laundry bag for dirty clothes on the way back.

## FAQ

**Is rolling really better than folding?**
For soft, casual clothes, yes — it saves space and reduces creases. Structured items (dress shirts, blazers) fold better.

**How do I avoid wrinkles?**
Fold structured items along seams, use packing cubes to stop shifting, and unpack soon after arriving.

## Conclusion

Plan a mix-and-match wardrobe, roll soft clothes, fold structured ones, and layer heavy items at the bottom. You'll fit more, stay organized, and arrive ready to go.
