You don't need a big budget to play great games. Free-to-play has come a long way, and some of the most popular PC titles cost nothing to start. Here's how to pick the right ones for you.

## How to choose a free game

Before downloading, weigh three things:

| Factor | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Genre you enjoy | Fun beats hype every time |
| Your PC's power | Some run on anything, some need a GPU |
| Monetization | Cosmetics = fair; pay-to-win = avoid |

## Best by category

### Competitive shooters
Fast, team-based, endlessly replayable. Great if you like quick matches and improving a skill. These usually need a semi-decent GPU.

### Battle royales
Big, social, and free to jump into. Perfect for playing with friends, and most run on modest hardware.

### MOBAs and strategy
Deep, long-term games with huge communities. A big time investment, but the depth is unmatched and they run on low-end PCs.

### Cozy and casual
Relaxing games for unwinding — low pressure, low system requirements, easy to pick up and put down.

> **Tip:** "Free" games make money somehow. Prefer ones that sell cosmetics over ones that sell power — pay-to-win ruins the fun fast.

## Step: Check your PC first

Before downloading, check the game's system requirements against your PC's CPU, RAM, and graphics. Many free games scale well, but competitive shooters run best with a dedicated GPU.

## FAQ

**Are free PC games actually good?**
Yes — many of the most-played games in the world are free-to-play with full-quality production.

**Will they ruin my PC with ads or malware?**
Stick to official stores and launchers. Avoid sketchy "free download" sites, which are the real risk.

## Conclusion

Pick a genre you enjoy, check it runs on your PC, and favor games with fair cosmetic monetization. There's a genuinely great free game for every kind of player.
