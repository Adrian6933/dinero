Low FPS makes games stutter and hurts your aim. These steps will boost frame rates on most PCs — from budget builds to high-end rigs.

## Step 1: Update your graphics drivers

Outdated GPU drivers are a common cause of poor performance. Update from your GPU maker's official app — new drivers often add big FPS gains for popular games.

## Step 2: Use the right in-game settings

These settings have the biggest impact on FPS for the smallest visual loss:

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Resolution | Native, or one step lower for more FPS |
| Shadows | Low/Medium (huge FPS impact) |
| Anti-aliasing | Low or off |
| View distance | Medium |
| V-Sync | Off (reduces input lag) |

## Step 3: Game-specific tips

- **PUBG:** Set shadows to Very Low and textures to Medium for a competitive edge.
- **Fortnite:** Use Performance Mode for the highest FPS.
- **Valorant:** Already runs well — lower shadows and turn off bloom for extra frames.

## Step 4: Optimize Windows for gaming

1. Enable **Game Mode** in Windows Settings.
2. Set your power plan to **High Performance**.
3. Close background apps before playing.

## Step 5: Keep your PC cool

Overheating causes throttling, which tanks FPS. Clean dust from your PC and make sure your fans work properly.

## FAQ

**Why is my FPS suddenly low?**
Common causes: a recent driver update, background apps, overheating, or a game patch. Check those first.

**Does lowering resolution really help?**
Yes — it's one of the most effective ways to gain FPS on weaker GPUs.

## Conclusion

Update drivers, lower shadows, turn off V-Sync, and keep your PC cool. Those four changes deliver the biggest FPS gains for most players.
