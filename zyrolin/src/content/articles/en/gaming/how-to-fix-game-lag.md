There are two kinds of "lag": low FPS (the game runs slowly) and network lag (online delay). This guide tackles both.

## First, identify the type

| Symptom | Type | Section |
|---|---|---|
| Choppy, low frame rate | Performance | Below |
| High ping, rubber-banding online | Network | Below |

## Fixing performance lag

1. **Update your GPU drivers** — often an instant improvement.
2. **Lower shadow and effects settings** — the biggest FPS killers.
3. **Close background apps** — browsers and chat apps eat resources.
4. **Set the game to High Performance** in Windows graphics settings.
5. **Check temperatures** — overheating causes throttling and stutter.

## Fixing network lag

1. **Use a wired Ethernet connection** instead of Wi-Fi.
2. **Close downloads and streams** using your bandwidth.
3. **Choose a server close to you** in the game's settings.
4. **Restart your router** to clear congestion.

## FAQ

**Why do I stutter even with high FPS?**
Often background apps, a full hard drive, or thermal throttling. Check temps and free up storage.

**Is Wi-Fi okay for online gaming?**
It works, but wired is far more stable and lower latency.

## Conclusion

Figure out whether it's FPS or network lag, then work the matching checklist. Driver updates, lower settings, wired internet, and cool temps fix the vast majority of cases.
