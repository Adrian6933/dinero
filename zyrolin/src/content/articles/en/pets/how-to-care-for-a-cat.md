Cats are independent but they still need the right care to thrive. Here's everything a new owner should know.

## Feeding

Feed a high-quality cat food appropriate for their age. Most adult cats do well with two meals a day. Always provide fresh water, and avoid giving milk — most cats are lactose intolerant.

## The litter box

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| One box per cat, plus one | Cats dislike sharing |
| Scoop daily | Cats avoid dirty boxes |
| Keep it quiet | Privacy matters |

## Grooming

Most cats groom themselves, but brushing weekly (or daily for long-haired cats) reduces hairballs and shedding. Trim nails every few weeks.

## Health basics

- Schedule annual vet check-ups.
- Keep up with vaccinations and parasite prevention.
- Watch for changes in appetite, litter habits, or energy — these are early warning signs.

## Keeping your cat happy

Cats need stimulation: scratching posts, climbing spots, and daily play. A bored cat can become destructive or stressed.

## FAQ

**How often should I take my cat to the vet?**
At least once a year for healthy adults; more for kittens and seniors.

**Should my cat go outside?**
Indoor cats live longer and safer lives. If they go out, ensure they're vaccinated and microchipped.

## Conclusion

Good food, a clean litter box, regular grooming, vet care, and daily play. Get those right and you'll have a healthy, happy cat.
