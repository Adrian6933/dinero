Updated graphics drivers fix bugs, improve performance, and add support for new games — sometimes adding a noticeable FPS boost. Here's how to update NVIDIA drivers the safe way.

## Step 1: Check your current driver

Right-click the desktop and open the NVIDIA Control Panel, or check **System Information** to see your current driver version.

## Step 2: Use the official app

Download the **NVIDIA App** (the successor to GeForce Experience) from NVIDIA's official website only. It detects your GPU and offers the latest driver.

> Never download drivers from third-party sites — they're a common malware source.

## Step 3: Choose the right driver

| Type | Best for |
|---|---|
| Game Ready Driver | Gamers (latest game support) |
| Studio Driver | Creators (stability for apps) |

## Step 4: Do a clean install

In the advanced options, choose **Clean installation**. This removes old driver files that can cause conflicts.

## Step 5: Restart

Restart your PC after installing so the new driver loads fully.

## FAQ

**A game broke after updating. What now?**
Roll back to the previous driver in Device Manager → Display adapters → Properties → Roll Back Driver.

**How often should I update?**
Before major game releases, or every couple of months for stability.

## Conclusion

Check your version, use the official NVIDIA app, pick the right driver, do a clean install, and restart. Your GPU will run smoother and faster.
