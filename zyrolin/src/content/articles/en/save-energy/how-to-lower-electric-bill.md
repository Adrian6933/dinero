Your electric bill is full of small leaks that add up. Fix them and you can save a meaningful amount every month — most cost nothing.

## Free changes (do these today)

1. Turn off lights and devices you're not using.
2. Unplug chargers and electronics in standby ("phantom" power).
3. Wash clothes in cold water.
4. Air-dry clothes instead of using the dryer.
5. Use natural light during the day.

## Low-cost upgrades

6. Switch to LED bulbs — they use up to 80% less energy.
7. Use power strips to cut standby power in one click.
8. Add weather stripping to seal drafts.
9. Use a programmable thermostat.

## Heating and cooling (your biggest cost)

10. Set the thermostat a couple of degrees lower in winter, higher in summer.
11. Clean or replace AC and heater filters regularly.
12. Close blinds during hot days and open them on sunny winter days.

## Where the savings come from

| Change | Typical saving |
|---|---|
| LED bulbs | 5–15% of lighting |
| Adjusting thermostat | 10%+ of heating/cooling |
| Killing standby power | 5–10% overall |

## FAQ

**What uses the most electricity at home?**
Heating, cooling, water heating, and the fridge are usually the biggest.

**Are LED bulbs really worth it?**
Yes — they cost more upfront but last years longer and use far less power.

## Conclusion

Start with the free habits, switch to LEDs, and tame your heating and cooling. A handful of small changes can noticeably shrink your bill.
