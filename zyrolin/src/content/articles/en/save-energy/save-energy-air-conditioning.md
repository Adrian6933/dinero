Air conditioning is often the biggest part of a summer electric bill. A few smart changes can cut that cost significantly while keeping you comfortable.

## The best temperature setting

The sweet spot for efficiency is around **24–26°C (75–78°F)**. Every degree cooler can increase energy use by roughly 6–8%.

> Raising your thermostat by just 1–2 degrees is the single easiest way to lower your cooling bill.

## Habits that save energy

1. **Use a fan with your AC.** Fans let you feel comfortable at a higher thermostat setting.
2. **Close blinds during the day** to block heat from the sun.
3. **Clean or replace filters** — a clogged filter makes the AC work harder.
4. **Don't cool empty rooms.** Close vents or doors where you can.

## Use a programmable thermostat

Set the temperature higher when you're asleep or away, and have it cool down shortly before you return. This avoids cooling an empty home.

## Seal the cool air in

Check for gaps around windows and doors. Sealing leaks keeps cool air inside and stops your AC from running constantly.

## How much can you save?

| Change | Estimated saving |
|---|---|
| Raise thermostat 2°C | 12–16% |
| Use fans + higher setting | 10%+ |
| Clean filters | 5–15% |

## FAQ

**Is it cheaper to leave the AC on all day?**
Usually no. Turning it up (not fully off) while you're out generally saves more than maintaining a cold house.

**Does closing vents in unused rooms help?**
It can, but don't close too many at once — it can strain some systems. Focus on doors and blinds first.

## Conclusion

Set your thermostat to 24–26°C, pair it with fans, block the sun, and keep filters clean. These habits cut cooling costs without sacrificing comfort.
